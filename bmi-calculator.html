Understanding BMI and GLP-1 Eligibility

BMI is one of several factors physicians consider when evaluating candidates for GLP-1 weight loss medication.

What BMI Means

Body Mass Index is a screening tool that estimates body fat based on height and weight. While widely used, it has important limitations.

  • Does not directly measure body fat percentage
  • May overestimate fat in muscular individuals
  • Does not account for age, sex, or ethnicity variations
  • Best used alongside other health assessments

BMI and GLP-1 Eligibility

FDA guidelines generally consider GLP-1 medications for adults who meet specific BMI thresholds along with other clinical criteria.

  • BMI of 30 or higher (obesity)
  • BMI of 27+ with weight-related conditions
  • Conditions include type 2 diabetes, hypertension, or high cholesterol
  • A physician makes the final determination

What Physicians Consider

Your doctor evaluates a comprehensive picture of your health beyond just BMI when prescribing GLP-1 medications.

  • Complete medical history and current medications
  • Blood work and metabolic health markers
  • Previous weight loss attempts
  • Family history and individual risk factors
